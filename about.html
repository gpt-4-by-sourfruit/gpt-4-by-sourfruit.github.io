About GPT-4

        
    GPT stands for Generative Pre-trained Transformer. In OpenAI's case its Generative Pre-trained Transformer 3. 
I was denied by OpenAI to access GPT-3s API, I was excited for it! So after being denied twice, I set off on my own.

    This starts at the end of June; and at this point, I have had around 4-5 years of experience with devloping AI.  
I gather data from lots of places, with it being all in all around ~2TiBs. And GPT-3.1 has reached a somewhat good alpha 
by the end of July/start of August. By the end of August I have trained GPT-3.1 on roughly 1.6TiBs of data. 
 By the end of August/start of September GPT-3.1 has reached beta! Fun, right? Well no. Bug fixes were made in a span of 2
weeks of no-sleep. By mid-September GPT-3.1 looked more promising than ever. Up until this point, the goal was to copy OpenAI.
But...... if I have GPT-3.1 that is better than GPT-3 already, why not go further? 

    Since then GPT-3.1 has learned 3 more languages in additon to its 2. 5 languages to program in; 2 supported languages to write text in:
English and Russian. Then for programming languages: Python, HTMl, CSS, Ruby, Javascript.* 

   
Now, this is GPT-3.1 talk, but now its the 27th of October, 2020. Welcome GPT-4. GPT-3.1 but better, by up to 3x.
